Get Yours

Simple.

ScanBand has two parts:

  • A rechargeable vibrating pod
  • A high-quality, washable headband

The headband's stitched-in compartment holds the pod, which slides out easily for charging.

The pod knows when your head turns. When you're scanning like Messi, it's happy; when you're not, it vibrates to remind you.

Why I Created ScanBand.

I'm Connor Owen, a 17-year-old D1 soccer player for Columbia and former San Jose Earthquakes Academy (MLS Next) player. I've always loved two things: creating and soccer.

When I was 12, I invented the Bookie Cushion, a bookmark that doubles as a whoopee cushion. I balanced the project with school and soccer. I made prototypes, had it manufactured, and eventually licensed it to Fred & Friends. It's now sold at places like Barnes & Noble and Walmart.

When I was 12, I was a solid soccer player but never the best player. I was on the B team at my old club.
I wanted to level up. I knew I wasn't the strongest or the fastest. But I obsessed over the details — especially the mental side of the game, and specifically scanning.

Things started to change.

National Recognition

I got invited to national team camps and all-star games. It was incredible.

College Recruitment

I was recruited by top D1 schools and committed to Columbia. Coaches consistently pointed to my field awareness and decision-making.

The Problem

As valuable as scanning was for me, it was one of the hardest things for me to improve. I noticed my teammates wanted to improve but struggled.

The Idea

That's when I realized I could combine my two passions, soccer and creating.

And the ScanBand was born.
