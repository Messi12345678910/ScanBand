My Story

I'm Connor Owen, a 17-year-old D1 soccer player for Columbia and former San Jose Earthquakes Academy (MLS Next) player. I've always loved two things: creating and soccer.

When I was 12, I invented the Bookie Cushion, a bookmark that doubles as a whoopee cushion. I balanced the project with school and soccer. I made prototypes, had it manufactured, and eventually licensed it to Fred & Friends. It is now sold at places like Barnes & Noble and Walmart.

At the same time, I was a solid soccer player, but never the best player. I was on the B team at my old club. I wanted to level up. I knew I wasn't the strongest or fastest. But I obsessed over the details — especially the mental side of the game and specifically scanning.

Things started to change.
